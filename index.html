Unicorn Companies Around The World (2007 - Present)

A unicorn company is a privately-held startup that has reached a valuation of over $1 billion. The term "unicorn" was coined to highlight the rarity of such companies, as achieving this valuation milestone is considered an extraordinary feat. These companies often operate in high-growth industries like technology, fintech, and healthcare, and they are known for their innovation and rapid market expansion. Unicorns play a significant role in shaping global markets and trends, as their disruptive business models and cutting-edge solutions attract substantial investor interest.

Number of Unicorn Companies for Each Country

The visualization reveals a clear concentration of unicorn companies in specific regions, with the United States leading by a significant margin, followed by China and India. These countries dominate the global startup ecosystem, reflecting their strong innovation and tech sectors. In contrast, many regions, particularly in Africa and South America, show little to no presence of unicorn companies, highlighting a global disparity in startup growth. This suggests that economic factors, technological infrastructure, and investment opportunities are driving the uneven distribution of high-value startups across the world.

Year of Companies Became Unicorn

The line chart shows the number of companies that became unicorns by year instead of their founding year, with a sharp spike in 2021, reaching nearly 500 companies. Prior to this, the growth was relatively steady but low, with gradual increases starting around 2015. The surge in 2021 is likely due to the economic and technological shifts brought on by the COVID-19 pandemic, which accelerated digital transformation, e-commerce, and fintech adoption, leading to a surge in startup valuations. After 2021, the number of unicorn companies dropped sharply, possibly due to market corrections and cooling investment trends.

Proportion of Unicorn Companies by Industries

Based on our observations of the pie chart, it is evident that before 2018, the consumer and retail industry held the largest portion of unicorn companies. However, in 2018, there was a significant shift, with enterprise tech taking the lead as the dominant sector. This marks a pivotal change, demonstrating that since 2018, investors and entrepreneurs have increasingly focused on the tech industry. The data further supports this, showing that the tech industry has consistently produced the most unicorn companies each year since 2018.
